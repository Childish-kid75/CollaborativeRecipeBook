# ?? Classic Shoyu Ramen 
 
## Description 
A savory Japanese noodle soup with a soy sauce-based broth, tender chashu pork, and perfectly cooked ramen noodles. 
 
## Ingredients 
 
### Broth 
- 2 lbs chicken backs or wings 
- 1 lb pork bones 
- 1 onion, halved 
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed 
- 1 inch ginger, sliced 
- 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces 
- 10 cups water 
 
### Chashu Pork 
- 1 lb pork belly 
- 1/2 cup soy sauce 
- 1/4 cup mirin 
- 1/4 cup sake 
- 2 tbsp sugar 
- 2 cloves garlic 
 
### Tare (Seasoning Base) 
- 1/2 cup soy sauce 
- 2 tbsp mirin 
- 1 tbsp sake 
- 1 tsp salt 
 
### Toppings 
- 4 packs fresh or dried ramen noodles 
- 4 soft-boiled eggs 
- 4 sheets nori (dried seaweed) 
- 1 cup corn kernels 
- 1 cup bamboo shoots (menma) 
- 2 green onions, sliced 
 
## Instructions 
 
1. **Make the broth:** Blanch bones in boiling water for 5 minutes, then drain and rinse. In a large pot, add bones, chicken, water, onion, garlic, ginger, and green onions. Bring to a boil, then reduce to simmer for 2 hours. Skim foam occasionally. 
 
2. **Prepare chashu pork:** Roll pork belly tightly and tie with kitchen twine. In a pot, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, garlic, and 1 cup water. Add pork and simmer on low for 1.5 hours. Remove pork and slice into 1/4-inch rounds. 
 
3. **Make marinated eggs:** Soft-boil eggs for 6.5 minutes, then transfer to ice bath. Peel eggs and marinate in leftover chashu liquid for 2 hours or overnight. 
 
4. **Prepare tare:** Combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, and salt in a small saucepan. Gently heat and stir until salt dissolves. Set aside. 
 
5. **Assemble the ramen:** Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions. In each bowl, pour 2 tbsp of tare. Ladle 1.5 cups of hot broth over tare. Add drained noodles. Arrange toppings: chashu slices, halved egg, nori, corn, bamboo shoots, green onions. 
 
## Tips 
- For a richer broth, simmer for 4-6 hours 
- Vegetarian? Replace meats with shiitake mushrooms and kombu
